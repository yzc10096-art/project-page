    <section id="abstract">
      <h2>Abstract</h2>
      <div class="grid-2">
        <!-- å·¦åˆ—ï¼šæ‘˜è¦æ–‡å­— -->
        <p>
         Depression, as a prevalent mental health disorder, requires an efficient, objective, and scalable approach to automated recognition.This paper presents a novel depression recognition framework named MGFKD (Multi-modal Gait Fusion with Knowledge Distillation), which jointly models RGB, infrared (IR) and depth gait data. MGFKD takes temporal gait templates as input and employs modality-specific subnetworks to extract features independently. These multi-modal features are concatenated along the channel dimension and further refined through a Transformer-based fusion module to enhance high-level representation learning. To effectively handle modality-missing scenarios, a parallel training mechanism for bi-modal and tri-modal configurations is introduced. Additionally, a knowledge distillation (KD) strategy based on minimizing the mean squared error between optimized features is incorporated to improve performance under bi-modal input conditions. Experimental results on a gait dataset comprising 300 healthy, 200 subthreshold, and 97 depressed individuals demonstrate the effectiveness of MGFKD in three-class depression recognition tasks, with superior performance in binary classification settings. Furthermore, a series of ablation studies validate the effectiveness of the proposed architectural components and training strategies.
        </p>
